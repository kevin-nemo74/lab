Intelligent System for Anatomopathological Diagnosis

Goal: To develop a diagnostic aid system based on AI techniques to address time and precision challenges.

Members: Pr. Hakim Bendjenna, Dr. Gasmi Mohamed, Mr. Ghenai Charaf Eddine (PhD student).

Starting Date: 2022 | Duration: 4 years

Full Description: Artificial intelligence has permeated all domains, especially those in which the human mind is strongly compelled to think and experiment. It represents a current topic in anatomical pathology, explaining the exponential increase in its involvement, particularly in medical detection and diagnosis. This will induce a future transformation of the pathologist profession and will change the role of anatomical pathology in the healthcare system. Since machines have demonstrated a level of maturity and accuracy in diagnostic results that surpasses that of a human being, we aim to develop a diagnostic aid system based on AI techniques to address the issues of time and precision. This project aims to utilize artificial intelligence to enhance and expedite the diagnosis of diseases through digitized slides of anatomical pathology. It will considerably ease patient management through the digitization of diagnostic processes based on the use of various antibodies. We propose a tele-diagnostic and expertise system among pathologists.

Early Detection of Neural Diseases via Manuscript Analysis

Goal: To develop a system for detecting neural diseases based on handwriting analysis.

Members: Dr. Taher Mekhaznia, Dr. Bennour Akram, PhD students: Asma Aoun Allah, Tag Samir, Souli Yassine, Mahmoudi Rachid, Benredjem Sabrina, Boudraa Merouane, Kaber Nour El Houda.

Starting Date: 2022 | Duration: 4 years

Full Description: Neural cells are irreplaceable, and the loss of control over muscles due to neural diseases leads to movement disorders. The project aims to detect early signs of diseases like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s through handwriting analysis. This approach allows early detection, preventing the disease's proliferation. Handwriting is sensitive to changes in human behavior due to depression, fatigue, etc., and can serve as an early detection tool for specialists.

Stabilization and Explosion of Solutions in Nonlinear Evolution Systems

Starting Date: 2021 | Duration: 4 years

Stabilization and Explosion of Solutions in Nonlinear Evolution Systems

Goal: To explore conditions that promote system stabilization towards equilibrium and investigate causes of system destabilization, including finite-time explosion.

Members: Pr. Mesloub Fatiha, Pr. Zarai Abderrahmane, Benzahi Mourad (PhD student), Merah Ahlem (PhD student), Toualbia Sarra (PhD student).

Starting Date: 2021 | Duration: 4 years

Full Description: The proposed problems constitute a major research axis. Initially, it involves testing existing techniques and methods on new problems that naturally arise in various application domains. Subsequently, improving conditions and extending results to other, more general situations by modifying existing methods, and potentially establishing new techniques capable of solving the problems under investigation. The question of local existence, while it may pose technical complications and serves as the initial step before proceeding further, in our opinion, does not necessarily constitute a major contribution. Rather, our primary interest lies in the preference for exponential stabilization of the system towards equilibrium and the conditions favoring system destabilization, particularly finite-time explosion.
